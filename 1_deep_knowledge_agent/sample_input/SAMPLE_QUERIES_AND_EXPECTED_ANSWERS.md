Query 2
Which scientist’s work linked water to the spread of disease, and how was this confirmed? Include the years, section numbers, and specific findings.

Answer:
Dr. John Snow, in 1854, mapped the London Broad Street cholera outbreak demonstrating contaminated water was the disease source [2.2, 4.1]. His findings revolutionized epidemiology by confirming the link between water supply and public health.
